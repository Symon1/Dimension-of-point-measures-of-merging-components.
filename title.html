Title                Dimension  of point measures of merging components.       Consider  a point measure, μ, which maps to an open interval of the real line.  Associate this measure with an unweighted instance of merging  components. x||y denotes the idea that x and y, although  distinguishable components, merge. The term "merging"  encompasses concepts like interleaving, overlapping, intersecting or  mixing.          A  question then arises for ratios μ0:,…,:μn-1,  of the size of n, the number of point measures, μ, required to  model each condition of an instance of merging components. The  property of instantaneity satisfies non-transitivity and symmetry  assumptions.           The  components are included within a generating multiset, S = {si,  sj, ...}. The space, Ω||,  of pairwise merges applied to S, generates a set, M, denoted by  indices of S. The following rules construct M:       ||1 ∀i,   si||si     ||2 ∀i∀j,  si||sj →∃Mij ∈  M       The  elements of M are mappings, {m(i), m(j),...} of indices of S.          Rules  satisfying these assumptions.       A0  ∀i∀j,  Mij= Ø       A1 ∀i∀j,   Mij = {m(i), m(j)}       A2 ∀i∀j∀k,  Mij = ∀m(k)|  si||sk  v  sj||sk,  i ≠k,  j≠k          A3 ∀i∀j∀k,  Mij = ∀m(k)|  si||sk  v  sj||sk       For  sets in M, i=j or i≠j,  for indices, Mij,  of M generated by ||1 and  ||2 respectively.  As such, n = 42  = 16 distinct conditions.  For μij,  i describes the rule Ai applied to sets generated by ||1, j describes  the rule Aj applied to sets generated by ||2.  If Ai=Aj, the 16 distinct  conditions reduce to four, μ00:μ11:μ22:μ33..  μ00, corresponding to the empty  set, has no representation, so the four ratios can be represented as  3-dimensional.
