Consider a point measure, μ, which maps to an open interval of the real line. Associate this measure with an unweighted instance of merging components without bias. x||y denotes the idea that x and y, although distinguishable components, merge. The term "merging" encompasses concepts like interleaving, overlapping, intersecting or mixing.
 
A question then arises for ratios μ0:,…,:μn-1, of the size of n, the number of point measures, μ, required to model each condition of an instance of merging components. The property of instantaneity satisfies non-transitivity and symmetry assumptions.  
The components are included within a generating multiset, S = {si, sj, ...}. The space, Ω||, of pairwise merges applied to S, generates a set, M, denoted by indices of S. Rules ||1 and ||2 construct M:
||1 ∀i, si||si
||2 ∀i∀j, si||sj →∃Mij ∈ M
The elements of M are mappings, {m(i), m(j),...} of indices of S.
Rules satisfying these assumptions.
A0 ∀i∀j, Mij= Ø

A2 ∀i∀j∀k, Mij = ∀m(k)| si||sk v sj||sk, i ≠k, j≠k

For sets in M, i=j or i≠j, for indices, Mij, of M generated by ||1 and ||2 respectively. As such, n = 42 = 16 distinct conditions. For μij, i describes the rule Ai applied to sets generated by ||1, j describes the rule Aj applied to sets generated by ||2.
