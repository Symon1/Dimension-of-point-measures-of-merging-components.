Consider a point measure, μ, which maps to an open interval of the real line.
Consider that this corresponds to an object of merging components. x||y denotes the idea that x and y, although distinguishable components, merge. The term "merging" encompasses concepts like interleaving, overlapping, intersecting or mixing.  
 
A question then arises for ratios μ0:,…,:μn-1, of the dimension of n, the number of point measures, μ, corresponding to each condition applied to an instance of merging components which are uniform in scale, satisfying properties of non-transitivity and symmetry.
The components are included within a generating multiset, S = {si, sj, ...}. The space, Ω||, of pairwise merges applied to S, generates a set, M, denoted by indices of S. Rules ||1 and ||2 construct M:
||1         ∀i, si||si
||2         ∀i∀j, si||sj →∃Mij ∈ M
The elements of M are mappings, {m(i), m(j),...} of indices of S.
The
A0         ∀i∀j, Mij= Ø
A1         ∀i∀j, Mij = {m(i), m(j)}
A2         ∀i∀j∀k, Mij = ∀m(k)| si||sk v sj||sk, i ≠k, j≠k  
A3         ∀i∀j∀k, Mij = ∀m(k)| si||sk v sj||sk
Rules A0, A1, A2 and A3 satisfy non-transitivity and symmetry. A0, by being empty, A1 by mapping elements named in the merge operation of a subset of M, A2 by containing maps of points in  closure of A1, A3 by containing A1 and its closure, A2, under the merge operation. Any rule containing a mapping from outside this closure would be transitive, showing these are sufficient.
